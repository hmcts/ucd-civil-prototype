Before you make a money claim

In some situations a money claim might not be your only or best option

Talk to the person who owes you

Before you make a claim it's a good idea to try to sort out the situation with the person who owes first.

  • Contact them - you can just speak to them, or send an email or letter if you’d like to have a record.
  • Tell them what you believe they owe you and why.
  • Explain that if you can't reach an agreement (such as a timetable for paying the money) you'll make an official money claim.
  • Tell them that they might have to pay the full amount as well as fees and other costs if they ‘defend the claim’, and that they could get a County Court Judgment (CCJ).

It could count against you in court if you don’t try to sort out the dispute with the person directly, before you make an official claim.

Mediation

A mediation service could be quicker and cheaper than going to court.

Mediation is when an impartial person helps both sides work out an agreement. You can often do this over the phone. And if you don’t want to speak to the person you're claiming against a mediator can speak to you separately.
