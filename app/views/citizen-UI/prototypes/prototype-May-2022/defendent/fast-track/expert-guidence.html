Using expert evidence

Expert evidence is an opinion based on the expertise of a professional.

This will only be allowed if the court is unable to make a decision without the assistance of a specialist. For example, a building surveyor who can comment on the quality of building work.

An expert can provide evidence:

  • in written form
  • in person, as a witness at the trial

Usually experts only give written evidence, but they may also appear as witnesses at a trial if:

  • each party has an expert
  • both experts disagree, and the court can only decide between their evidence by hearing it in person

For more information about using an expert for evidence, read the Civil Procedure Rules for experts and assessors..
